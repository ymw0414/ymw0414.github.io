Event study: Share of Republican-leaning articles, by year

Trade Shocks and Media Slant

Working Paper

This paper studies how local exposure to NAFTA-era trade shocks shaped U.S. newspaper coverage of trade policy. Combining a large corpus of local news articles with a shift-share research design, I measure changes in media slant on trade-related issues and examine their downstream effect on political attitudes and voting behavior.

Hours elasticity of labor supply, by rider experience

In and Out: How Do Food Delivery Riders Work?

with Jungmin Lee and Jinseong Park

Working Paper

We estimate the wage elasticity of labor supply among workers with highly flexible schedules — food delivery riders. Using unique real-time, transaction-level data spanning nearly two years, we find that a one percent increase in the expected hourly wage raises the probability of working by 0.7–0.9 percentage points. Intensive-margin wage elasticities range from 2.5 to 2.8. Riders' labor supply becomes more elastic as they accumulate experience, and we find suggestive evidence that riders learn to reduce delivery time per order with experience.

Presented at: Washington Area Labor Economics Symposium (WALES) 2026 [Poster].
