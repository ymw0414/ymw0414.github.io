Event study: Share of Republican-leaning articles, by year

Trade Shocks and Media Slant

Working Paper

  • Question. Does trade liberalization reshape the political language of local news?
  • Data. 52 million U.S. local newspaper articles (1987–2004) and U.S. congressional speeches.
  • Identification. Event-study DiD with a shift-share design exploiting cross-CZ variation in NAFTA tariff vulnerability.
  • Finding. Newspapers in the most trade-exposed areas became 9.6 percentage points more Republican-leaning than those in the least exposed; the shift concentrates in initially Democratic-leaning papers in low-education areas, consistent with newspapers adjusting to changing reader preferences.

Hours elasticity of labor supply, by rider experience

In and Out: How Do Food Delivery Riders Work?

with Jungmin Lee and Jinseong Park

Working Paper (draft available upon request)

  • Question. How elastic is labor supply for gig workers with flexible schedules?
  • Data. 21 million transaction-level records from a Korean food-delivery platform (2019–2021).
  • Identification. Shift-share IV using municipality-level restaurant category shares interacted with daily national category-specific demand.
  • Finding. A 1% rise in the expected hourly wage raises hours worked by 2.5–2.8% and the probability of working by 0.7–0.9 pp; both responses grow stronger with rider experience. We also find suggestive evidence of learning by doing: with experience, riders bundle orders and select routes more efficiently, lowering delivery time per order.
