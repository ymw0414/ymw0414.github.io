*with Jungmin Lee (Seoul National University) and Jinseong Park (KDI School of Public Policy and Management)*

**Abstract.** We estimate the wage elasticity of labor supply among workers with highly flexible schedules — food delivery riders. Using unique real-time, transaction-level data on food delivery activity spanning nearly two years, we find that a one percent increase in the expected hourly wage increases the probability of working by 0.7–0.9 percentage points. Estimates of the intensive-margin wage elasticity range from 2.5 to 2.8. At both the extensive and intensive margins, riders' labor supply becomes more elastic as they accumulate experience. We find suggestive evidence that riders learn to reduce delivery time per order with experience.
